Wu Zi - Art of War (Wu Qi)
==================================================

Chapter 1 - Planning for the State

Wu Qi said: In ancient times, those who governed states always first instructed the people and cherished the masses. There are four conditions of disharmony: discord within the state - the army cannot march; discord within the army - battle formations cannot be deployed; discord in formations - the advance cannot press forward; discord in battle - victory cannot be decided.

Wu Qi said: The Tao returns all things to their source and start. Righteousness accomplishes affairs and establishes merit. Planning avoids harm and seeks advantage. The essentials preserve achievement and maintain success. If conduct is not in accord with the Tao, if actions are not in accord with righteousness, yet one occupies high position and great nobility, calamity will certainly reach them.

Wu Qi said: Victory lies in order. If laws and orders are not clear, rewards and punishments not trustworthy, though you have a million soldiers - what use are they? Those who achieve order: in residence, they have propriety; in movement, they inspire awe; in advance, they cannot be stopped; in retreat, they cannot be pursued. They are called the armies of parent and child.

Wu Qi said: On the battlefield where armies contend, where corpses lie - those who face death with certainty will live; those who hope for life will die. The skilled commander is like one sitting in a leaking boat or crouching in a burning building - the wise have no time to scheme, the brave have no time to anger. Thus they can receive the enemy.

Wu Zi said: People constantly die from what they cannot do, and fail at what they are not accustomed to. Therefore in the methods of war, instruction comes first. One man learns war - ten are taught. Ten men learn war - a hundred are taught. Thus the whole army is trained. Take positions near and await those far; rest and await the fatigued; the fed await the hungry.
