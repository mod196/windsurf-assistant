DK 1. Though this Logos is eternally valid, yet men are unable to understand it — not only before hearing it, but even after they have heard it for the first time.
万物皆遵逻各斯而行，然人们不能理解——无论在听闻之前还是初次听闻之后。

DK 2. Though the Logos is common, the many live as if they had a wisdom of their own.
逻各斯虽共有，多数人却各行其是，如同各有私智。

DK 10. Things taken together are wholes and not wholes; what is being brought together and brought apart; what is in tune and out of tune; out of all things one, and out of one all things.
合则为全，分则为散；和则为谐，离则为异；万物出于一，一出于万物。

DK 12. Upon those who step into the same rivers, different and ever different waters flow down.
踏入同一条河流的人，始终有不同的水流向他们。

DK 30. This world-order, the same for all, no god nor man has made, but it ever was and is and will be: an ever-living fire, kindling in measures and going out in measures.
这个世界秩序，对一切存在者皆同，既非任何神所造，也非任何人所造；它过去是、现在是、将来也是一团永恒的活火，按照一定的分寸燃烧，按照一定的分寸熄灭。

DK 31. The turnings of fire: first sea; and of the sea, half is earth, half firestorm.
火之转化：先为海；海之半为土，半为烈风。

DK 36. For souls it is death to become water; for water it is death to become earth. From earth comes water, and from water soul.
灵魂变水即死，水变土即死。由土生水，由水生魂。

DK 41. Wisdom is one thing: to know the thought by which all things are steered through all things.
智慧在于一件事：认识那主宰一切的思想。

DK 44. The people must fight for their law as for their wall.
人民当为法律而战，如为城墙而战。

DK 45. You would not find out the boundaries of soul, even by traveling along every path: so deep a logos does it have.
你走遍所有的路也找不到灵魂的边界：它的逻各斯如此之深。

DK 49a. We step and do not step into the same rivers; we are and are not.
我们踏入又不踏入同一条河流；我们存在又不存在。

DK 50. Listening not to me but to the Logos, it is wise to agree that all things are one.
听从逻各斯而非听从我，承认万物为一乃是智慧。

DK 51. They do not comprehend how a thing agrees at variance with itself; it is an attunement turning back on itself, like that of the bow and the lyre.
人们不理解总是如此者如何与自身相异：反张之和谐，如弓与琴。

DK 53. War is the father of all and king of all; and some he shows as gods, others as men; some he makes slaves, others free.
战争是万物之父，万物之王。它使一些人成为神，另一些人成为人；使一些人成为奴隶，另一些人成为自由人。

DK 54. The hidden harmony is better than the visible.
隐秘的和谐胜于显明的和谐。

DK 56. People are deceived in the knowledge of visible things in the same way as Homer, who was the wisest of all the Greeks.
人们对可见之物的认识被欺骗，如荷马——全希腊最有智慧的人。

DK 58. Good and evil are one. Physicians who cut and burn complain that they receive no worthy fee for doing it.
善与恶为一。正如医生切割烧灼病人，而索取与疾病无异之酬劳。

DK 60. The way up and the way down is one and the same.
上升之路与下降之路，一而同之。

DK 62. Immortals are mortal, mortals immortal; living the others' death, dying the others' life.
不死者为有死者，有死者为不死者。此之生即彼之死，彼之生即此之死。

DK 64. Thunderbolt steers all things.
雷霆驾驭一切。

DK 67. God is day-night, winter-summer, war-peace, satiety-famine. He changes as fire, when mixed with spices, is named according to each.
神是昼夜、冬夏、战争与和平、饱满与饥饿。他变化如火：与香料混合时，随各物之名而被命名。

DK 80. One must know that war is common, and right is strife, and all things come into being through strife and necessity.
须知斗争即正义，万物皆通过斗争和必然性而生成。

DK 88. The same thing in us is living and dead, waking and sleeping, young and old; for these change and become those, and those change back and become these.
生与死、醒与睡、少与老——皆为同一。前者变而为后者，后者变而复为前者。

DK 90. All things are an exchange for fire, and fire for all things, as goods for gold and gold for goods.
万物由火生，火由万物生，犹如金由物换，物由金换。

DK 91. You cannot step twice into the same river; for other waters are ever flowing on to you.
你不能两次踏入同一条河流，因为新的水不断流过你。

DK 101. I searched for myself.
我寻找过我自己。

DK 102. To God all things are beautiful and good and just; men, however, think some things unjust and others just.
对神而言一切美好、善良、正义；人们则妄分善恶。

DK 103. In a circle, beginning and end are common.
圆上，始与终为一。

DK 108. Of all whose discourses I have heard, none has arrived at this: knowing that wisdom is apart from all.
我听过所有人的言论，无人达到这一点：认识到智慧独立于一切之外。

DK 112. Sound thinking is the greatest excellence. Wisdom is to speak what is true and to act according to nature, giving heed to it.
健全的思考是最大的优点。智慧是说出真实并按照自然而行。

DK 119. Character is destiny for man.
人的性格就是他的命运。

DK 121. Every Ephesian grown man should hang himself, and leave the city to the boys.
以弗所人人都该自缢，把城邦留给孩子。

DK 123. Nature loves to hide.
自然喜欢隐藏自己。

DK 125a. Even the posset separates if it is not stirred.
混合饮料不搅动就会分离。

DK 126. Cold things grow warm; warm grows cold; wet grows dry; dry grows wet.
冷变暖，暖变冷；湿变干，干变湿。

DK B6 (Arius). The sun is new every day.
太阳每天都是新的。

DK B52. Lifetime is a child playing, moving pieces in a game. Kingship belongs to a child.
时间是一个玩骰子的儿童；王权属于儿童。

Selected Additional Fragments (Burnet 1920)

Frag. 17: The many do not perceive what is right before them, nor do they understand when instructed; yet they think they do.
大多数人对身边事物视若无睹，即便经人指教亦不能理解，只是自以为理解罢了。

Frag. 19: Eyes and ears are bad witnesses to men having barbarian souls.
眼与耳对于灵魂粗蛮之人乃是坏证人。

Frag. 21: All that we see when awake is death; all that we see asleep is sleep.
清醒者共有一个世界，睡眠者各有各的世界。

Frag. 29: The best choose one thing above all others: immortal glory among mortals. The many stuff themselves like cattle.
最优者选择一物超于一切：不朽的荣耀。众人如牛般饱食。

Frag. 43: Presumption must be quenched even more than a conflagration.
傲慢比烈火更须熄灭。

Frag. 73: We must not act and speak as though asleep.
我们不应如沉睡者般行事言说。

Frag. 78: Human nature has no insight, but the divine has.
人性无洞见，唯神性有之。

Frag. 114: Those who speak with understanding must hold fast to what is common to all.
以理性言说者必须持守万物之共同。

Frag. 121: Abundance of knowledge does not teach understanding.
知识之丰富不教人智慧。
