======================================
DEFINITIONS (定义)
======================================

I. By that which is SELF-CAUSED, I mean that of which the essence involves existence, or that of which the nature is only conceivable as existent.
【自因者：其本质即含存在，其本性只能被构想为存在的。】

II. A thing is called FINITE AFTER ITS KIND, when it can be limited by another thing of the same nature.
【有限者：能被同类事物所限制。】

III. By SUBSTANCE, I mean that which is in itself, and is conceived through itself: in other words, that of which a conception can be formed independently of any other conception.
【实体：在自身中并通过自身被构想——其概念不依赖任何他物之概念。】

IV. By ATTRIBUTE, I mean that which the intellect perceives as constituting the essence of substance.
【属性：理智所认知的构成实体本质的东西。】

V. By MODE, I mean the modifications of substance, or that which exists in, and is conceived through, something other than itself.
【样态：实体的变状，在他物中存在并通过他物被构想。】

VI. By GOD, I mean a being absolutely infinite—that is, a substance consisting in infinite attributes, of which each expresses eternal and infinite essentiality.
【神：绝对无限的存在——由无限属性构成的实体，每一属性均表达永恒无限的本质。】

VII. That thing is called FREE, which exists solely by the necessity of its own nature, and of which the action is determined by itself alone.
【自由者：仅因自身本性之必然而存在，其行动由自身单独决定。】

VIII. By ETERNITY, I mean existence itself, in so far as it is conceived necessarily to follow solely from the definition of that which is eternal.
【永恒：存在本身——就其被理解为必然地仅从永恒之物的定义中推出而言。】

======================================
AXIOMS (公理)
======================================

I. Everything which exists, exists either in itself or in something else.
【凡存在者，或在自身中存在，或在他物中存在。】

II. That which cannot be conceived through anything else must be conceived through itself.
【不能通过他物被构想者，必须通过自身被构想。】

III. From a given definite cause an effect necessarily follows; and if no definite cause be granted, it is impossible that an effect can follow.
【从给定的确定原因，结果必然随之而来；无确定原因则无结果。】

IV. The knowledge of an effect depends on and involves the knowledge of a cause.
【对结果的认识依赖并包含对原因的认识。】

V. Things which have nothing in common cannot be understood the one by means of the other.
【无共同点之物，不能相互理解。】

VI. A true idea must correspond with its ideate or object.
【真观念必与其对象相符。】

VII. If a thing can be conceived as non-existing, its essence does not involve existence.
【若一物可被构想为不存在，其本质不包含存在。】

======================================
KEY PROPOSITIONS (核心命题)
======================================

Prop. V. There cannot exist in the universe two or more substances having the same nature or attribute.
【宇宙中不能存在两个或多个具有相同本性或属性的实体。】

Prop. VII. Existence belongs to the nature of substances.
【存在属于实体的本性。】

Prop. XI. God, or substance, consisting of infinite attributes, of which each expresses eternal and infinite essentiality, necessarily exists.
【神，或由无限属性构成的实体，必然存在。】

Prop. XIV. Besides God no substance can be, nor can be conceived.
【除神之外，没有实体能够存在，也不能被构想。】
—— Corollary I: God is one, that is, only one substance can be granted in the universe.
—— Corollary II: Extension and thought are either attributes of God or accidents of the attributes of God.

Prop. XV. Whatsoever is, is in God, and without God nothing can be, nor can be conceived.
【凡存在者皆在神中，离开神，任何事物既不能存在也不能被构想。】

Prop. XVI. From the necessity of the divine nature must follow an infinite number of things in infinite ways—that is, all things which can fall within the sphere of infinite intellect.
【从神之本性之必然，必然推出无限数量的事物，以无限多种方式。】

Prop. XVIII. God is the immanent, and not the transitive, cause of all things.
【神是万物的内在原因，而非外在（他动）原因。】

Prop. XXIX. Nothing in the universe is contingent, but all things are conditioned to exist and operate in a particular manner by the necessity of the divine nature.
【宇宙中无偶然——一切事物皆由神性本质之必然所决定而存在和运作。】

Appendix (核心段落):
"Men are mistaken in thinking themselves free; their opinion is made up of consciousness of their own actions, and ignorance of the causes by which they are conditioned."
【人误以为自己自由——此见解不过是对自身行为的意识加上对其条件原因的无知。】
